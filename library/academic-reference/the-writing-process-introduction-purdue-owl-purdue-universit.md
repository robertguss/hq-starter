 These OWL resources will help you with the writing process: pre-writing (invention), developing research questions and outlines, composing thesis statements, and proofreading. While the writing process may be different for each person and for each particular assignment, the resources contained in this section follow the general work flow of pre-writing, organizing, and revising. For resources and examples on specific types of writing assignments, please go to our Common Writing Assignments area. 
## In this section

*   ### [Writing Task OWL Resource List This resource will help you find OWL material for the many different kinds of writing tasks you may face in school and in the workplace.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/writing_task_resource_list.html)
*   ### [Invention: Starting the Writing Process Tips for how to start a writing assignment.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/invention_starting_the_writing_process.html)
*   ### [Organization & the CARS Model](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/organization_CARS_Model.html)
*   ### [Tips and Examples for Writing Thesis Statements This resource provides tips for creating a thesis statement and examples of different types of thesis statements.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/thesis_statement_tips.html)
*   ### [Reverse Outlining: An Exercise for Taking Notes and Revising Your Work This exercise is useful for either difficult texts that you must read, or as a way to revise your work for organization and clarity.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/reverse_outlining.html)
*   ### [Time Management: Conquering Long Assignments](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/time_management_conquering_long_assignments.html)

## Subsections

*   ### [Prewriting (Invention) This section explains the prewriting (invention) stage of the composing process. It includes processes, strategies, and questions to help you begin to write.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/prewriting/index.html)
*   ### [Writer's Block A description of common causes of writer’s block and advice for overcoming these blocks. Written by Kylie Regan.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/writers_block/index.html)
*   ### [Stasis Theory This resource provides an overview of stasis theory and what you can do with it to help you conduct research, compose documents, and work in teams.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/stasis_theory/index.html)
*   ### [Developing an Outline This resource describes why outlines are useful, what types of outlines exist, suggestions for developing effective outlines, and how outlines can be used as an invention strategy for writing.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/developing_an_outline/index.html)
*   ### [Proofreading Proofreading is primarily about searching your writing for errors, both grammatical and typographical, before submitting your paper for an audience (a teacher, a publisher, etc.). Use this resource to help you find and fix common errors.](https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/general_writing/the_writing_process/proofreading/index.html)
