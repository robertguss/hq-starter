## Here's what growing on Substack actually looks like right now

### You're posting into the void

You write a Note, hit publish, and... hope. You have no idea which Notes bring subscribers, which ones flop, or what to do differently. Substack shows you almost nothing useful.

### Your notifications are a warzone

80 new notifications. Some are comments you should reply to. Some are likes from yesterday. Some are replies to other people's threads. Good luck finding what matters.

### You have no idea when your audience is reading

You have great notes and ideas, but you can't know when your audience will read them.

### Substack scheduler is too basic

It's not possible to batch schedule/unschedule your Notes, or import new ones, and managing them is almost impossible.

### Engaging with your community takes forever

You want to reply to comments, check in on creators you follow, keep conversations going. But between the messy feed and the scattered notifications, it takes hours to do what should take minutes.

### You're doing all of this on top of writing

The Notes, the engagement, the analytics guesswork, the consistency pressure. It's not one thing that's hard — it's everything stacking up until you burn out.
