## The Best Way to Get Started on Youtube

…is to make a video _today_ using whatever gear you have on hand.

You almost certainly have a 4K-capable camera in your pocket – it’s called your phone. And many people are running 7-figure channels just filming videos on their phone.

> “Camera gear doesn’t matter, just how you use it.” – Casey Neistat

Additionally, the only way you’ll get better as a YouTuber is by making lots of videos. This is a world-class collection of information; many of the resources here are the exact ones I’ve used along my own journey.

But to progress, you have to get your hands dirty and make videos. Godspeed!

## Who am I?

![Image 22: Thomas Frank - Headshot 2023](https://thomasjfrank.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/01/Thomas-Frank-Headshot-2023.jpg)

Hi! I’m [Thomas Frank](https://thomasjfrank.com/), and I’ve been a full-time YouTuber and content creator since 2012.

I’ve built two successful YouTube channels: [Thomas Frank](https://www.youtube.com/@thomasfrank), which focuses on productivity and self-improvement (2.9 million subscribers), and [Thomas Frank Explains](https://www.youtube.com/@thomasfrankexplains), which is a hyper-niche channel all about Notion (179,000 subscribers).

I run a business selling [Notion](https://thomasjfrank.com/payment-platforms-tool/) templates that [makes around $100,000/mo](https://twitter.com/TomFrankly/status/1523730922633191424) on average. I’m also a co-founder and owner of [Nebula](https://nebula.tv/), a creator-owned streaming service and [talent agency](https://talent.nebula.tv/).

I have two main professional passions:

1.   Getting hyper-technical and learning new skills
2.   Sharing what I know

Over the last 10 years I’ve spent as a full-time creator, my obsessive personality has pushed me down many rabbit holes. As a result, I’ve learned a lot about filmmaking, audio engineering, music production, online marketing, programming, and other topics.

One of my long-term goals is to share as much of what I’ve learned for free. I’ve long been inspired by the [MDN Web Docs](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/), and someday I’d like to turn this resource into something that looks similar. For now, I’m building it MVP-style and improving it over time in public.

Here’s some of the work I’m most proud of – which may also serve as a bona-fides section that can bolster the information and advice you’ll find in the rest of the guide.
