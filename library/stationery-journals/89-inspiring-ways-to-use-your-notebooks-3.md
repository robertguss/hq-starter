Nathalie Valmary, author Why this e-book 

Through my brand, Louise Carmen, I’ve had the opportunity to talk with 

several thousand  people who use notebooks. I noticed that notebook users 

are not ordinary people. 

Generally speaking, they’re very creative in understanding their lives. They 

have a common tendency of avoiding trends, a passion for the arts, respect 

for people, discovery and other 

cultures. 

One day, a friend told me, "You should write a book about all the ways 

your clients use their notebooks. It's really interesting!” 

It’s from this reflection that the book, 89 Inspiring Ways to Use Your 

Notebooks, was born. 

Some of the ways may seem a bit wacky or may challenge you, but they’ll 

also give you ideas. This list is not exhaustive. I encourage you to help add 

to it by sending me a message. 

I’m Nathalie Valmary, and I’m co-founder of the Louise Carmen brand.

# Table of contents For feeling good 3. success notebook 

in this notebook, write down all the good 

decisions you’ve made, the goals you’ve 

been  focusing on and what you’ve 

achieved. Maybe you’ve helped someone 

or you’ve finally mastered your 

grandmother’s cake recipe, etc. Writing 

down each day’s successes will help you 

feel more self-confident. Too often, we 

only focus on the negatives or on our 

failures. ... 

2. anti-bullshit notebook 

this is your outlet, where you write down 

all your negative thoughts so you can get 

them out.  You may discover that they’re 

ridiculous, fun or something worth 

exploring. 

4. notebook of unsent letters 

write letters to those you have something 

to say. You’re never going to send them. 

This is to  help you get things out and so 

that one day, you can re-read them. 

a date with yourself 1. 

It’s your time — that which you reserve on 

your schedule for spending time alone. Choose 

a place that inspires you or where you feel at 

ease. A park, a café or your home. This time 

allows you to take stock of what you’ve 

accomplished and to decide what you’d like to 

do. It’ll give you a lot of self-confidence and 

will allow you to connect with everything 

positive you’ve done, which is extremely 

important in order to grow. 5. reenergizing phrases 

In a notebook, write down all the phrases, 

mantras and affirmations that motivate 

you. This is  the notebook you’ll enjoy 

reading when you need to pump up your 

morale. 

6. visual gratitude notebook 

this is a gratitude notebook, but instead of 

writing, you’re going to draw things, 

make collages,  do sketches and paste 

photos. 

7. brain relaxation 

Are you trying to focus on a subject, but 

your brain is all over the place? Open up 

your  notebook and write down everything 

that comes into your head, no matter 

what it is. This will calm your soul. You’ll 

come back to it later. 

8. divorce journal 

This may seem like a strange type of 

notebook, but it can be very useful and 

rich with learning.  Write about your 

breakup and your state of mind in the 

days following. How you transcended 

certain moments, etc. ... 9. gratitude notebook 

Each day, write down 3 or 4 positive 

things about that day. Write about the 

simple pleasures  you created for yourself. 

Even if they seem insignificant, they allow 

you to be aware of them and reconnect 

with the positive. 

10. life notebook 

Whenever you need to, write freely about 

the things that bother you, your successes, 

your  failures, your fears, your dreams, etc. 

This could also be called your intimate 

notebook. S t a r t o u t s m a l l  . d r e a m b i g

# Travel notebooks 15. travel planning notebook 

This is a notebook about your dream 

vacation. It allows you to organize your 

future travels in a visual way, like a

vacation notebook, but without knowing 

whether you’ll one day go. Paste maps, 

write down places you’d like to discover: 

museums, boutiques, cafés, restaurants 

and markets, their hours, their specialities, 

etc. Don’t forget basic vocabulary so you 

can be understood  !

16. vacation notebook 

This one’s about remembering those 

fleeting moments. It allows you to collect 

memories all  throughout your travels. 

Like good wines, they become more and 

more valuable with the passing of time. 

It’s more about preserving memories and 

emotions than about artistic performance !17. van life 

do you love the freedom that comes with 

taking trips in a van or camper? Write 

down the best  spots for sleeping and how 

to get there, as well as the facilities and 

ambiance, provisions and good times, 

etc. …

18. vacation address notebook 

This is your bible with all the restaurants, 

hotels, concept stores and little bistros 

discovered  during your travels. Describe 

the decor, the welcome you received, 

whether reservations were required and 

signature dishes. Basically everything you 

can share with friends or what you'd like 

to remember...so that one day, you can 

return.

# F a m i l y 20. moms and dads notebook 

there’s no user’s manual for parents, so 

why not make your own? Write down 

your  experiences as parents as well as 

your solutions. You can refer back to 

them or share them with your friends. 

Write down all the fun little everyday 

things (or the challenges) having to do 

with your role as a parent. This notebook 

allows you to go back and find tips, 

names of books, destinations and games 

or activities to do with your kids. ... 

21. pregnancy journal for expectant 

fathers 

Who says fathers can’t keep a pregnancy 

journal? Here, he can recount his 

challenges,  thoughts, joys, how he felt 

during ultrasound appointments, 

preparation for birth, how he felt seeing 

mom’s belly grow, etc. He can also insert 

photos of each month with a little 

explanation and all the things he’ll one 

day share with his child. 23. Family reunion notebook 

This is your guestbook for times spent 

with family. You can paste photos, ask 

everyone to draw something or write a 

short message. It’ll be a nice collection of 

good times shared with family. 

22. pregnancy notebook for mothers 

Ultrasounds, your daily routine, photos of 

you in your favorite outfits, baby names, 

nursery  decorating ideas, the music you’re 

listening to, doctor appointments, 

breathing exercises, photos in profile of 

you each month...you’ll be happy to 

revisit all this in a few years and share 

it with your child. 24. baby notebook 

This is the notebook you create for your 

child to give to them on their 18th 

birthday. Write down  stories, highs and 

lows, add photos, collages, his or her 

drawings, handprints or footprints on 

each birthday and thoughts for your child. 

Write him or her letters that you’ll keep in 

an envelope. Attached it to the notebook. 

We no longer have rites of passage. 

Offering him or her this notebook on 

their 18th birthday is a lovely gesture. 

You can begin this notebook at any time, 

even if they’re no longer a baby. 

25. grandchildren’s book 

Create a notebook for each of your 

grandchildren. Write down recipes you 

make with/for him  or her. Write down the 

words to songs you sing to him or her and 

all the little things you want to pass on. 

Paste pictures with him or her throughout 

the years. Talk about the vacations you’ve 

taken together over the years. Don’t hold 

back; these are all the things they’ll 

appreciate when they’re older. 26. Youki notebook 

Youki is your dog, your cat or your 

guinea pig. Paste photos, paw prints 

throughout the years, 

stories, the mischief they get into, your 

worries, that time they ran away...because 

Youki is a valued member of the family. Precious memories 27. mythical journeys 

Each evening, in the hotel or in your tent, 

talk about your day, the countryside, your 

thoughts and your emotions. At the end of 

the road, you’ll discover the micro-

transformations of each day through the 

emotions you relay. Draw something even 

if you can’t draw, collect plants and leave 

them in your notebook to dry. You can 

paste them as in a herbarium. 

29. your long walks 

Keep a daily diary of your walks. Write 

down what you experienced, felt and saw. 

Add stories.  If you’re walking a known 

route, talk about each phase. Draw a quick 

diagram of the route. Note the number of 

kilometers, the differences in altitude, who 

or what you’ve encountered, noteworthy 

places with their geolocations, etc. 

Remember to make a checklist of things 

not to forget and to note down any extras 

that you shouldn’t have taken and that only 

weigh down your bag... 28. month after month 

Each month, as part of a ritual, write down, 

draw or paste a photo of the highlights of 

that  month. At the end of the year, you’ll 

have a wonderful summary of your year. 

You’ll see that your year has been very 

eventful. 30. memory notebook 

Tell the stories of your grandparents and 

great grandparents along with everything 

you know  about your family. Ask your 

grandparents questions about their parents. 

Talk about your childhood, your 

upbringing and the values your family 

passed on to you. If you have photos or 

drawings, paste them inside to help 

illustrate. This is a notebook of great value 

that you can pass on to your descendants. 32. wedding notebook 

Plan your wedding with this notebook and 

keep it for the rest of your life. Write about 

the  places where you’d like to celebrate 

your wedding, your dress, your guests, 

table decorations, announcement ideas, etc. 

The ideal is to have three 60-page 

notebooks and to center each one around a 

theme: an inspiration notebook, a planning 

notebook and a guest notebook, where 

you’ll ask your guests to leave a nice note. 

The Louise Carmen planning notebook or 

the Magnum are perfect for this.

# A healthy body 33. exercise notebook 

Note your achievements and challenges, 

how you treated your injuries, describe 

your feelings  and your progress. Map out 

your exercises. Put together your weekly 

program by indicating the type of exercise 

you’ll do each day. For example, Monday: 

yoga, Tuesday: arms, Wednesday: jogging, 

etc. You’ll gradually end up with a bible 

that’s rich in insights about yourself. 

35. meal tracking notebook 

every day, write down what you’ve eaten 

at each meal, how much water you drank 

and how  you felt at the end the meal: full, 

not too full or still hungry. Create your 

own visual codes for understanding 

everything at a glance. 

34. health tracking notebook 

write down your number of steps each day, 

the number of hours you slept, your 

weight, etc. If  you keep a nutrition 

notebook, the goal is to write down 

everything each day on a double page. 36. healing recipes 

In a number of Asian countries and in

fighting colds or flus, etc. Also write down 

the foods to avoid. 

37. healthy recipe notebook 

Write down the healthy recipes you’ve 

enjoyed. Write down how you felt as well 

as improvements for the next time you 

make them. Indicate where you purchased 

your ingredients.. 

38. grandma’s beauty recipes
