# Better notes → Better thinking

![Image 1: Duly Noted](https://dulynoted.fyi/assets/images/duly-noted-perspective.webp)

**Notes extend your mind.** They offload your memory and allow you to think through complex issues. But there's more to notes than you may think.

_Duly Noted_ shows you tools, practices, and frameworks for thinking more effectively with digital notes. You’ll become a better reader, writer, learner, teacher, parent, worker — a better human.

## The Author

[Jorge Arango](https://jarango.com/) is an information architect, author, and educator. For almost three decades, he has architected digital experiences and made the complex clear for organizations ranging from non-profits to Fortune 500 corporations.

He is the author of _[Living in Information: Responsible Design for Digital Places](https://rosenfeldmedia.com/books/living-in-information/)_ and co-authored _[Information Architecture: For the Web and Beyond](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/information-architecture-4th/9781491913529/?\_gl=1*163ob7a*\_ga*MjExMzc2MzYzLjE2OTkwMjkyNzY.*\_ga\_092EL089CH*MTcwMTU3NjQwMy4yLjEuMTcwMTU3NjQzMC4zMy4wLjA.)_, the fourth edition of O'Reilly's celebrated Polar Bear book.

In addition to his design consulting practice, Jorge hosts [_The Informed Life_ podcast](https://theinformed.life/), writes [a blog](https://jarango.com/blog), and teaches at the [California College of the Arts](https://www.cca.edu/design/interaction-design/).
