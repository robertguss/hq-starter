**Sir Demis Hassabis** (born 27 July 1976)[[6]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-AAA_bio-6) is a British [artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") (AI) researcher and entrepreneur. Jumper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_M._Jumper "John M. Hassabis is a [Fellow of the Royal Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow_of_the_Royal_Society "Fellow of the Royal Society") and has won awards for his research efforts, including the [Breakthrough Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakthrough_Prize "Breakthrough Prize"), the [Canada Gairdner International Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada_Gairdner_International_Award "Canada Gairdner International Award") and the [Lasker Award](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lasker_Award "Lasker Award"). He was appointed a [CBE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire "Order of the British Empire") in 2017, and [knighted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knight_Bachelor "Knight Bachelor") in 2024 for his work on AI.[[14]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-14) He was also listed among the [_Time_ 100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_100 "Time 100") most influential people in the world in 2017 and 2025, and was one of the "Architects of AI" collectively chosen as _Time_'s 2025 [Person of the Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time_Person_of_the_Year "Time Person of the Year").

## Career and research

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=4 "Edit section: Career and research")]

### Lionhead

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=5 "Edit section: Lionhead")]

After graduating from Cambridge, Hassabis worked at [Lionhead Studios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lionhead_Studios "Lionhead Studios").[[35]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-desert-35) Games designer Peter Molyneux, with whom Hassabis had worked at Bullfrog Productions, had recently founded the company. ### Elixir Studios

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=6 "Edit section: Elixir Studios")]

Hassabis left Lionhead in 1998 to found [Elixir Studios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elixir_Studios "Elixir Studios"), a London-based independent games developer, signing publishing deals with [Eidos Interactive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eidos_Interactive "Eidos Interactive"), [Vivendi Universal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vivendi_Universal "Vivendi Universal") and [Microsoft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microsoft "Microsoft").[[36]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-demishassabis.com-36) In addition to managing the company, Hassabis served as executive designer of the games _[Republic: The Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic:\_The\_Revolution "Republic: The Revolution")_ and _[Evil Genius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evil\_Genius\_(game) "Evil Genius (game)")._[[25]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-fifteen-25) Each received [BAFTA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BAFTA "BAFTA") nominations for their interactive music scores, created by [James Hannigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Hannigan "James Hannigan").

The release of Elixir's first game, _[Republic: The Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic:\_The\_Revolution "Republic: The Revolution")_, a highly ambitious and unusual political simulation game,[[37]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-BBC-37) was delayed due to its huge scope, which involved an AI simulation of the workings of an entire fictional country. The final game was reduced from its original vision and greeted with lukewarm reviews, receiving a [Metacritic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metacritic "Metacritic") score of 62/100.[[38]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-38)[Evil Genius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evil_Genius_(game) "Evil Genius (game)"), a tongue-in-cheek [Austin Powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austin_Powers "Austin Powers") parody, fared much better with a score of 75/100.[[39]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-39) In April 2005 the intellectual property and technology rights were sold to various publishers and the studio was closed.[[40]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Rebellion_Acquires_Vivendi_Licenses-40)[[41]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-IGN_Elixir_Studios-41)

### Neuroscience research

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=7 "Edit section: Neuroscience research")]

[![Image 5](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/PhotonQ-Demis_Hassabis_on_Artificial_Playful_Intelligence_%2815366514658%29_%282%29.jpg/250px-PhotonQ-Demis_Hassabis_on_Artificial_Playful_Intelligence_%2815366514658%29_%282%29.jpg)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/File:PhotonQ-Demis_Hassabis_on_Artificial_Playful_Intelligence_(15366514658)_(2).jpg)

Hassabis (left) with [Blaise Agüera y Arcas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blaise_Ag%C3%BCera_y_Arcas "Blaise Agüera y Arcas") (right) in 2014, at the [Wired](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wired_(magazine) "Wired (magazine)") conference in London

Following Elixir Studios, Hassabis returned to academia to obtain his [PhD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PhD "PhD") in [cognitive neuroscience](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitive_neuroscience "Cognitive neuroscience") from [UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UCL_Queen_Square_Institute_of_Neurology "UCL Queen Square Institute of Neurology")[[42]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-42) in 2009 supervised by [Eleanor Maguire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eleanor_Maguire "Eleanor Maguire").[[2]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-phd-2) He sought to find inspiration in the [human brain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_brain "Human brain") for new AI algorithms.[[43]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-43)

He continued his neuroscience and artificial intelligence research as a [visiting scientist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visiting_scholar "Visiting scholar") jointly at [Massachusetts Institute of Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Institute_of_Technology "Massachusetts Institute of Technology") (MIT), in the lab of [Tomaso Poggio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomaso_Poggio "Tomaso Poggio"), and [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University"),[[18]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-eveningstandard-18) before earning a [Henry Wellcome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Wellcome "Henry Wellcome")[postdoctoral research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postdoctoral_research "Postdoctoral research")[fellowship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fellow "Fellow") to the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit at UCL in 2009 working with [Peter Dayan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_Dayan "Peter Dayan").[[44]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-standard-44)

Working in the field of imagination, [memory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memory "Memory"), and [amnesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amnesia "Amnesia"), he co-authored several influential papers published in [_Nature_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nature_(magazine) "Nature (magazine)"), [_Science_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_(magazine) "Science (magazine)"), [_Neuron_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuron_(journal) "Neuron (journal)"), and _[PNAS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PNAS "PNAS")_.[[45]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-gs-45) His very first academic work, published in _PNAS_,[[46]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-46) was a landmark paper that showed systematically for the first time that patients with damage to their [hippocampus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hippocampus "Hippocampus"), known to cause amnesia, were also unable to imagine themselves in new experiences. The finding established a link between the constructive process of [imagination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imagination "Imagination") and the reconstructive process of [episodic memory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Episodic_memory "Episodic memory") recall. Based on this work and a follow-up [functional magnetic resonance imaging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Functional_magnetic_resonance_imaging "Functional magnetic resonance imaging") (fMRI) study,[[47]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-47) Hassabis developed a new theoretical account of the episodic memory system identifying _scene construction_, the generation and online maintenance of a complex and coherent scene, as a key process underlying both memory recall and imagination.[[48]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-48) This work received widespread coverage in the mainstream media[[49]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Amnesiacs_May_Be_Cut_Off_From_Past_and_Future_Alike|-49) and was listed in the top 10 scientific breakthroughs of the year by the journal _[Science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science\_(journal) "Science (journal)")_.[[50]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Breakthrough_of_the_Year-50) He later generalised these ideas to advance the notion of a 'simulation engine of the mind' whose role it was to imagine events and scenarios to aid with better planning.[[51]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-51)[[52]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-52)

### DeepMind

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=8 "Edit section: DeepMind")]

Hassabis is the CEO and co-founder of [DeepMind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_DeepMind "Google DeepMind"), a [machine learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Machine_learning "Machine learning") AI startup, founded in London in 2010 with [Shane Legg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shane_Legg "Shane Legg") and [Mustafa Suleyman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mustafa_Suleyman "Mustafa Suleyman"). Hassabis met Legg when both were [postdocs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postdoctoral_researcher "Postdoctoral researcher") at the Gatsby Computational Neuroscience Unit, and he and Suleyman had been friends through family.[[53]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-53) Hassabis also recruited his university friend and Elixir partner [David Silver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Silver_(computer_scientist) "David Silver (computer scientist)").[[54]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-54)

DeepMind's mission is to "solve intelligence" and then use intelligence "to solve everything else".[[55]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-55) More concretely, DeepMind aims to combine insights from systems neuroscience with new developments in machine learning and computing hardware to unlock increasingly powerful general-purpose learning algorithms that will work towards the creation of an [artificial general intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_general_intelligence "Artificial general intelligence") (AGI). The company has focused on training learning algorithms to master games, and in December 2013 it announced that it had made a pioneering breakthrough by training an algorithm called a Deep Q-Network (DQN) to play [Atari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atari "Atari") games at a superhuman level by using only the raw pixels on the screen as inputs.[[56]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Space_Invaders-56)

DeepMind's early investors included several high-profile tech entrepreneurs.[[57]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Angel_Profile-57)[[58]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Google_Buy-58) In 2014, [Google](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google "Google") purchased DeepMind for £400 million. Although most of the company has remained an independent entity based in London,[[59]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Reuters_DeepMind-59) DeepMind Health has since been directly incorporated into [Google Health](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Google_Health "Google Health").[[60]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-60)

Since the Google acquisition, the company has notched up a number of significant achievements, perhaps the most notable being the creation of [AlphaGo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo "AlphaGo"), a program that defeated world champion [Lee Sedol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lee_Sedol "Lee Sedol") at the complex game of [Go](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Go_(game) "Go (game)"). Go had been considered a holy grail of AI, for its high number of possible board positions and resistance to existing programming techniques.[[61]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-61)[[62]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-62) However, AlphaGo beat European champion [Fan Hui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fan_Hui "Fan Hui") 5–0 in October 2015 before [winning 4–1 against former world champion Lee Sedol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo_versus_Lee_Sedol "AlphaGo versus Lee Sedol") in March 2016[[63]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-In_a_Huge_Breakthrough_Google's_AI_Beats_a_Top_Go_Player-63)[[64]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-A_Google_Computer_Victorious_Over_the_World's_Go_Champion-64) and [winning 3–0](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaGo_versus_Ke_Jie "AlphaGo versus Ke Jie") against the world's top-ranked player [Ke Jie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ke_Jie "Ke Jie") in 2017.[[65]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-65) Additional DeepMind accomplishments include creating a [neural Turing machine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neural_Turing_machine "Neural Turing machine"),[[66]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Google's_Secretive_DeepMind_Startup_Unveils_a_Neural_Turing_Machine-66) reducing the energy used by the cooling systems in Google's data centres by 40%,[[67]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-67) and advancing research on AI safety.[[68]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Google_Developing_Kill_Switch_for_AI-68)[[69]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Newsweek_Big_Red_Button-69)

DeepMind has also been responsible for technical advances in machine learning, having produced a number of award-winning papers. In particular, the company has made significant advances in [deep learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_learning "Deep learning") and [reinforcement learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reinforcement_learning "Reinforcement learning"), and pioneered the field of deep reinforcement learning which combines these two methods.[[70]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-Deep_RL-70) Hassabis has predicted that artificial intelligence will be "one of the most beneficial technologies of mankind ever" but that significant ethical issues remain.[[71]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-71)

Hassabis has also warned of the potential dangers and [risks of AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Risks_of_AI "Risks of AI") if misused, and has been a strong advocate of further AI safety research being needed.[[72]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-72) In 2023, he signed [the statement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statement_on_AI_risk_of_extinction "Statement on AI risk of extinction") that "Mitigating the risk of extinction from AI should be a global priority alongside other societal-scale risks such as pandemics and nuclear war".[[73]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-CAIS-Statement-73) He considers however that a pause on AI progress would be very hard to enforce worldwide, and that the potential benefits (e.g. for health and against climate change) make it worth continuing. He said that there is an urgent need for research on evaluation tests that measure how capable and controllable new AI models are.[[74]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-74)

#### AlphaFold

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Demis_Hassabis&action=edit&section=9 "Edit section: AlphaFold")]

In 2016, DeepMind turned its artificial intelligence to [protein structure prediction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protein_structure_prediction "Protein structure prediction"), a 50-year grand challenge in science, to predict the 3D structure of a protein from its 1D [amino acid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amino_acid "Amino acid") sequence. This is an important problem in biology, as proteins are essential to life, almost every biological function depends on them, and the function of a protein is thought to be related to its structure. Knowing the structure of a protein can be very helpful in drug discovery and disease understanding. In December 2018, DeepMind's tool [AlphaFold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AlphaFold "AlphaFold") won the 13th [Critical Assessment of Techniques for Protein Structure Prediction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Critical_Assessment_of_Techniques_for_Protein_Structure_Prediction "Critical Assessment of Techniques for Protein Structure Prediction") (CASP) by successfully predicting the most accurate structure for 25 out of 43 proteins. "This is a lighthouse project, our first major investment in terms of people and resources into a fundamental, very important, real-world scientific problem", Hassabis said to _[The Guardian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The\_Guardian "The Guardian")_.[[75]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-75)

Hassabis at 2024 Nobel Week

In November 2020, DeepMind again announced world-beating results in the CASP14 edition of the competition with AlphaFold 2, a new version of the system. It achieved a median [global distance test](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_distance_test "Global distance test") (GDT) score of 87.0 across protein targets in the challenging free-modelling category, much higher than the same 2018 results with a median GDT < 60, and an overall error of less than the width of an atom (<1 [Angstrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angstrom "Angstrom")), making it competitive with experimental methods, and leading the organisers of CASP to declare the problem essentially solved.[[76]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-76)[[77]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-77) Over the next year DeepMind used AlphaFold2 to fold all 200 million proteins known to science, and made the system and these structures openly and freely available for anyone to use via the [AlphaFold Protein Structure Database](https://alphafold.ebi.ac.uk/) developed in collaboration with [EMBL-EBI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Bioinformatics_Institute "European Bioinformatics Institute").[[78]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demis_Hassabis#cite_note-78)
