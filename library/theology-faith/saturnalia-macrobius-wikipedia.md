_**Saturnalia**_ ([Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_language "Latin language"): _Saturnaliorum Libri Septem_, "Seven Books of the [Saturnalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia "Saturnalia")") is a work written after c. 431 CE by the Roman provincial [Macrobius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macrobius "Macrobius").[[1]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-macrobius_new-1) The _Saturnalia_ consists of an account of the discussions held at the house of [Vettius Agorius Praetextatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vettius_Agorius_Praetextatus "Vettius Agorius Praetextatus") during the holiday of the [Saturnalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia "Saturnalia"). It contains a great variety of curious historical, mythological, critical, [antiquarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiquarian "Antiquarian") and grammatical discussions.

_Saturnalia_ is a late example of the [Symposium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symposium_(ancient_Greece) "Symposium (ancient Greece)") genre pioneered by [Plato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symposium_(Plato) "Symposium (Plato)") and [Xenophon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symposium_(Xenophon) "Symposium (Xenophon)").[[2]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-SL-2) It is written as a series of scholarly dialogues at fictional banquets held over the eve of [Saturnalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia "Saturnalia") and three days of the [holiday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holiday "Holiday"), December 16–19.[[3]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-3) In each book, one of the characters does the bulk of the speaking on the topic.[[4]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-EB1911-4)

## Principal Characters

[[edit](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Saturnalia_(Macrobius)&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Principal Characters")]

Macrobius placed characters from different times and places in an [anachronistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anachronism "Anachronism") gathering. Some are historical figures, primarily from the 4th century in the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire"). Others are fictional or very loosely associated with obscure figures.[[5]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-Loeb-5): xxvi–xxxvi

*   **Praetextatus**: [Vettius Agorius Praetextatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vettius_Agorius_Praetextatus "Vettius Agorius Praetextatus"), a wealthy [pagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pagan "Pagan") aristocrat.
*   **Flavianus**: [Virius Nicomachus Flavianus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virius_Nicomachus_Flavianus "Virius Nicomachus Flavianus"), vicar of Africa, [prefect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Praetorian_prefecture_of_Italy "Praetorian prefecture of Italy") and [consul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consul "Consul").
*   **Symmachus**: [Quintus Aurelius Symmachus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quintus_Aurelius_Symmachus "Quintus Aurelius Symmachus"), orator, author, and politician.
*   **Decius**: Caecina Decius Albinus Iunior, [prefect of Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_urban_prefects_of_Rome#5th_century "List of urban prefects of Rome") and father of [Caecina Decius Aginatius Albinus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caecina_Decius_Aginatius_Albinus "Caecina Decius Aginatius Albinus").
*   **[Rufius Albinus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceionius_Rufius_Albinus "Ceionius Rufius Albinus")**: prefect of Rome.
*   **Eustathius**: Greek philosopher.
*   **Evangelus**: an aristocrat from [Tibur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tivoli,_Lazio "Tivoli, Lazio").
*   **Avienus**: a young aristocrat.
*   **Eusebius**: a Greek teacher of [rhetoric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhetoric "Rhetoric").
*   **[Servius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Servius_the_Grammarian "Servius the Grammarian")**: a [grammarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philology "Philology").
*   **Dysarius**: a physician.
*   **[Horus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horus_(athlete) "Horus (athlete)")**: a former boxer and [Cynic philosopher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cynicism_(philosophy) "Cynicism (philosophy)").

The first book is devoted to an inquiry as to the origin of the Saturnalia and the festivals of [Janus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janus_(mythology) "Janus (mythology)"), which leads to a history and discussion of the Roman calendar, and to an attempt to derive all forms of worship from that of the Sun.[[6]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-6) The second book begins with a collection of _bons mots_, to which all present make their contributions, many of them being ascribed to Cicero and [Augustus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augustus "Augustus"); a discussion of various pleasures, especially of the senses, then seems to have taken place, but almost the whole of this is lost. The third, fourth, fifth and sixth books are devoted to Virgil, dwelling respectively on his learning in religious matters, his rhetorical skill, his debt to [Homer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homer "Homer") (with a comparison of the art of the two) and to other Greek writers, and the nature and extent of his borrowings from the earlier [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") poets. The latter part of the third book is taken up with a dissertation upon luxury and the [sumptuary laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumptuary_law "Sumptuary law") intended to check it, which is probably a dislocated portion of the second book. The seventh book consists largely of the discussion of various physiological questions.[[4]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-EB1911-4)

The primary value of the work lies in the facts and opinions quoted from earlier writers. The form of the _Saturnalia_ is copied from [Plato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plato "Plato")'s _Symposium_ and [Gellius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aulus_Gellius "Aulus Gellius")'s _Noctes atticae_; the chief authorities (whose names, however, are not quoted) are Gellius, [Seneca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seneca_the_Younger "Seneca the Younger") the philosopher, [Plutarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plutarch "Plutarch") (_Quaestiones conviviales_), [Athenaeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athenaeus "Athenaeus") and the commentaries of [Servius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurus_Servius_Honoratius "Maurus Servius Honoratius") and others on Virgil.[[4]](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saturnalia_(Macrobius)#cite_note-EB1911-4)
