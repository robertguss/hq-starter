# Manuscript Submissions

P&R Publishing is dedicated to publishing excellent books that promote biblical understanding and godly living as summarized in the Westminster Confession of Faith and Catechisms. Our titles range from popular books designed to help lay readers grow in Christian thought and service to academic works advancing biblical and theological scholarship. Our mission is to serve Christ and his church by producing clear, engaging,and insightful applications of Reformed theology to life.

If you are a writer in the Reformed tradition who is interested in submitting a book idea or manuscript in keeping with our mission, P&R Publishing welcomes hearing from you. We are always interested in books that approach the Christian life and doctrinal topics in fresh, thoughtful, positive, and practical ways while maintaining a solid Reformed perspective. Our books are often addressed to pastors, counselors, and laypeople.

We encourage you to acquaint yourself with our online catalog before submitting a proposal.

**P&R does not publish:**

*   Art and photography books
*   Contemporary Fiction
*   Descriptions of angel or spirit encounters, near-death experiences, or visions
*   Fictionalized Bible stories
*   Memoirs
*   Poetry Collections
*   Prophecy or “Bible code” books
*   Romance novels
*   Screenplays

**How to submit your proposal:**

*   In order to make a formal proposal, you must download and fill out our [**submission form**](https://storage.googleapis.com/prp-shared/SubGuide2025.docx).
*   To submit a children's book, download and fill out [**this submission form**](https://storage.googleapis.com/prp-shared/ChildrensContentSubGuide2024.docx).
*   All forms must be submitted in Microsoft Word or Rich Text format and sent as attachments by e-mail to [[email protected]](https://www.prpbooks.com/cdn-cgi/l/email-protection#701113010519031904191f1e0330000200121f1f1b035e131f1d). Please use the subject line: Book Proposal, “[title]”, [author]
*   You can expect a response to your proposal within three months of its submission.

Any expression of interest should not be understood as an offer to publish. While P&R is always looking for qualified new authors, the publication of unsolicited submissions is rare.

We will not respond to, or return, proposals submitted by mail.
