_alphaXiv x marimo_

We’re challenging you to do what [alphaXiv](https://alphaxiv.org/) and [marimo](https://marimo.io/) both believe in: turn research results into something tangible, reproducible, and interactive — that is, a marimo notebook.

In this competition, you will participate in AI research by implementing a result in a marimo notebook. Pick from a [curated selection of papers](https://www.alphaxiv.org/shared/folder/019d4c1f-46d6-7aa6-8836-3856837453c6) on AlphaXiv and build a marimo notebook that brings its core idea to life.

We don’t expect you to fully reproduce your selected paper’s results, nor do we expect you to reimplement its entire methodology. Instead, we encourage you to hone in on a key contribution, and bring that contribution to life with code, UI elements, and explanatory text. Bonus points if you add your own spin on the paper’s method, such as an extension or variant of the algorithm, or applying it to different datasets. Others who work through your notebook should walk away with an intuitive understanding of the paper’s core contribution, and an understanding of how to implement and apply it themselves.

For an example of the kind of submission we’re looking for, see [this notebook on Sketch Vectorization](https://molab.marimo.io/github/rambip/sketch-vectorization/blob/1c5377984b0f3bd207aa636739ac94366708fff9/notebooks/walkthrough.py/wasm) by Antonin Peronnet, which not only re-implements the paper but also adds novel extensions such as a CNN with synthetic data augmentation. To learn what is possible with marimo, see [our gallery](https://marimo.io/gallery).

You will submit your notebooks as a [molab](https://molab.marimo.io/) link in the form submission below.

**Note:** In this competition, you’ll be limited to CPU compute. Stay tuned for a future competition in which we’ll provide GPUs…

## [](https://marimo.io/pages/events/notebook-competition#who-you-are)Who you are

Engineers, students, and researchers who are interested in AI research. Both the research-curious and veteran researchers are welcome.

## [](https://marimo.io/pages/events/notebook-competition#how-to-enter)How to enter

1.   Pick from a [curated selection of papers on alphaXiv](https://www.alphaxiv.org/shared/folder/019d4c1f-46d6-7aa6-8836-3856837453c6), or bring one you’re already curious about. Any alphaXiv paper is eligible.
2.   Build a marimo notebook that presents the core idea.
3.   Submit by sending us the molab link through the link below.

Individual or team submissions welcome. Submit one molab link per team and include all team members’ names in the form. You may submit up to 3 entries total. Each submission can be from a different team or individual — you’re not required to submit all entries under the same team or person.

## [](https://marimo.io/pages/events/notebook-competition#judging-criteria)Judging criteria

Submissions are reviewed by a panel of alphaXiv and marimo team members (2 each, 4 total). The jury favors:

*   Notebooks that provide an intuitive understanding of the research result, through code, UI elements, and text
*   Notebooks that implement custom extensions that either improve the performance of the original paper or otherwise provide insight into the topic at hand
